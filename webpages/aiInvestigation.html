A critique on a generative AI's book review on The Stranger

Written for: FYSE 1067A: Information State Across Ages

Written on October 9, 2023

Generative artificial intelligence, referred to as AI, is one of the newer emerging technologies that have taken the world by storm over the past year. AI has become so large that there has been an issue with it being used in schools to write full papers for students. For this paper I was asked to critique a book review done by generative artificial intelligence in which I attached the conversation between me and the AI at the end, I decided to ask the AI to review Albert Camus’ The Stranger. The Stranger is known for its depictions of absurdism and existentialism and is one of the most renowned novels of the 20th century. As someone who has read the novel twice and constantly thinks about some of the ideas brought up, I feel that I would be able to critique a review of the novel accurately.

The AI review gives some background to the novel including themes, when the book was first published, and comments about Camus’ style. The AI went as far as to describe Camus’ prose as “spare yet evocative” (ChatGPT 2023, line 4). Which I would say is pretty accurate as Camus stylistically chooses to use what feels like minimalistic language throughout the entirety of the novel. The AI also speaks highly of Camus’ descriptive language as the AI claims “[o]ne of the novel's most striking aspects is Camus' vivid portrayal of the Algerian landscape.” (ChatGPT 2023, line 17). I would not say that the description of the Algerian landscape is “[o]ne of the novel's most striking aspects” (ChatGPT 2023, line 17). It is one of the repeating aspects of the novel as it is one of the motifs of the novel, but it is something that I acknowledged and glanced over in my readings. The AI is accurate in saying that there is a symbiosis between the setting and Meursault’s, the protagonists’, issues as we see Meursault make irrational decisions while being in the presence of the sun. One positive about the AI review is that it mentions the spare prose and then mentions one of the moments when it strays away from the spareness for stylistic reasons.

The AI describes Meursault and briefly mentions that Meursault’s mother, Maman, dies at the beginning. Then it goes on to state that after Maman’s death there “is a sequence of events that expose the absurdity of existence.” (ChatGPT 2023, lines 7-8). It is more like the death of Maman is what starts the sequence rather than the sequence starting after his mother's death. The AI pays little attention to the death of Maman despite this death setting up the rest of the novel as Meursault is critiqued by society for not grieving his mother’s death and for even putting her in the home in the first place. The death of Maman also frames part two as during his case they mention Meursault’s lack of emotions towards his mother's death, even though part two of the novel is subjectively more “spare” than the first part it is just as important it only gets mentioned in one sentence throughout this entire AI review.

The AI then decided to dedicate two paragraphs to Camus’ exploration of the concept of the absurd. The AI states “that life is inherently devoid of meaning, and individuals must confront the absurdity of their existence head-on.” (ChatGPT 2023, lines 22-24). This is accurate as well, throughout the novel we see Meursault decide what is or is not important to him. The AI review briefly mentions how Meursault’s relationships are a mirror of him and mentions the characters Marie, Raymond, and Meursault’s defense attorney.

One thing that the AI review is missing is the inclusion of quotes or at least examples from the novel. For example, the AI mentions the Algerian sun making Meursault act irrationally but does not give any examples of it. The same goes for the sequence of events that “expose the absurdity of existence.” (ChatGPT 2023, line 8). The same can also be said for Meursault confronting the absurdity of reality. Quotes or at least examples could help to show the reader what you are talking about in the review. It would be beneficial to the reader to have an example of, let's say, the sun making Meursault act irrationally. Another missing piece of the AI review is any mention of part two of the novel. As mentioned before the AI review has only one sentence that references part two of The Stranger. It kind of feels like the AI only read the first part and decided that was enough to write a review on.

The AI review provided a decent review of what felt like only the first half of Albert Camus’ The Stranger. One shortcoming of the AI is that it may not have the right interpretation of the novel. One possible limitation of the AI is that it is unsure of how to either make quotes or take examples from the novel. Overall the AI review felt like a draft of a review that was done by someone who only read half of the book and did not include quotes yet. Generative artificial intelligence has a long way to go before it is the perfect review machine as of right now it makes the perfect half-done drafts.
